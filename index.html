Intro

Welcome to my e-portfolio. This website introduces my academic background, interests, career goals, and engineering projects. I created this portfolio as part of my university e-portfolio assignment, but I also want it to serve as a professional record of my growth as an engineering student.

My academic interests are centered on semiconductor devices, microelectronics, and circuit technology. I am especially interested in understanding how device-level physics connects to real electrical behavior, and how that knowledge can be applied to modern semiconductor systems.

About Me

I am currently studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech. My interest in engineering grew from a deeper curiosity about how electronic systems work beyond their visible functions. Rather than seeing devices only as finished products, I became interested in the principles behind them, especially how voltage, current, materials, and structure interact to create real electronic behavior.

Over time, this curiosity developed into a stronger focus on semiconductor devices and circuits. I enjoy learning both the theoretical and practical sides of engineering, and I try to connect classroom concepts to projects and technical exploration. I am particularly drawn to fields where physical understanding and system-level thinking meet, such as semiconductor device engineering, microelectronics, and hardware design.

This portfolio reflects both my technical interests and my long-term direction as an engineering student. My goal is not only to complete coursework successfully, but also to keep building the knowledge, projects, and communication skills needed for a strong career in the semiconductor industry.

Career Goals

My long-term goal is to become a semiconductor device engineer. I am especially interested in roles related to semiconductor devices, microelectronics, testing, and hardware-focused engineering. I want to build a career that combines strong physical understanding, analytical thinking, and practical engineering problem solving.

In the short term, I want to strengthen my foundation in device physics, circuit analysis, and electronics through coursework and hands-on projects. I also want to continue building projects that demonstrate both technical depth and the ability to explain engineering work clearly. Gaining internship experience in semiconductor-related roles is another important step in my development.

In the long term, I hope to contribute to semiconductor technology through device-level and hardware-level work. Whether in device characterization, manufacturing, testing, or related engineering areas, I want to grow into an engineer who can connect theory, measurement, and real-world application effectively.
