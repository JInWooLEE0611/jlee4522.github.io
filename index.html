About Me

I am currently studying Electrical Engineering at Georgia Tech. My interest in engineering developed from a deeper curiosity about how electronic systems operate beyond their visible functions. Rather than viewing devices only as finished products, I became interested in the underlying principles behind them, especially how voltage, current, materials, and structure interact to produce real electronic behavior.

Over time, this curiosity evolved into a stronger focus on semiconductor devices and circuits. I enjoy learning both the theoretical foundations and the practical aspects of engineering, and I actively try to connect classroom concepts to hands-on projects and technical exploration.

This portfolio represents my ongoing effort to document my learning process and technical growth. It reflects both my academic interests and the direction I am steadily building toward as an engineering student.

Career Goals

My long-term goal is to become a semiconductor device engineer. I am especially interested in roles related to semiconductor devices, microelectronics, testing, and hardware-focused engineering.

In the short term, my goal is to strengthen my understanding of semiconductor devices and the physical principles that govern electronic systems. I intentionally chose to focus on both circuit technology and electronic devices because I believe strong device-level knowledge is essential for the semiconductor engineering. In addition to this foundation, I am also interested in gaining introductory experience in VLSI and digital design during my undergraduate studies. Although my primary focus remains in semiconductor devices, exploring CMOS-based system design will provide valuable insight into how devices are used in real-world hardware systems.

In the long term, I hope to establish myself as a semiconductor device engineer who combines deep device-level understanding with system-level and digital design awareness. By connecting theory, measurement, and design, I aim to contribute to semiconductor technology through practical, hardware-centered engineering work.
